FREE CONCERTS
BY OSESP 2022

Since its first concert, in 1954, the Orquestra Sinfônica do Estado de São Paulo — Osesp — has built a highly successful trajectory. The Orchestra is today an inseparable part of São Paulo and Brazilian culture, promoting profound cultural and social transformations.

Starting from the basic idea that the Orquestra Sinfônica do Estado de São Paulo – Osesp could, in addition to promoting musical performances of high artistic quality, play a fundamental role in the process of cultural diffusion and democratization of classical music, the Osesp Foundation created in 2008 the Osesp Itinerante series, a program that travels through the most diverse cities in the interior of São Paulo with a series of free musical activities, hoping to reach an audience that would hardly have access to the Orchestra concerts that regularly take place at Sala São Paulo and in the capital of São Paulo.
The eight free concerts will take place in two blocks of simultaneous presentations, with two orchestras in each city, two conductors (the trombonist Wagner Polistchuk and the timpanist Ricardo Bologna, who also act as musicians for Osesp), and two soloists (the violinist Soraya Landim and cellist Jin Joo Doh, who are also members of the Orchestra), each performing in four different cities. The repertoire of the eight performances will feature works by Beethoven (such as Symphonies No. 2 and No. 4) and Saint-Saëns (such as his Suite for Cello and Orchestra).

The OSESP brand redesign was awarded a silver star at the 35th Festival of the São Paulo Creative Club. The São Paulo State Symphony Orchestra has always been affectionately called 'OSESP' by the public. The problem is that the previous brand only read 'OSP'. The solution was to simply use the name by which it was called in the brand, in addition to simplifying its design.
